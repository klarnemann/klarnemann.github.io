about me

I was born and raised in Fairview Park, Ohio (a small, blue-collar subarb of Cleveland). In 2010, I recieved my B.A. from Case Western Reserve University, majoring in Cognitive Science and in Philosophy. As an undergraduate, I worked as a research assistant with Dr. Anthony Jack, studying individual differences and the distinct fMRI activation profiles of social and non-social reasoning. When I graduated, my now-husband James and I moved to Berkeley, California. I conducted postbac research with the US Department of Veteran's Affairs in affiliation with UC Berkeley under Dr. Mark D'Esposito and Dr. Anthony Chen, studying the rehabilitation of executive dysfunction in brain-injured patients using approaches from MVPA and graph theory. Presently, I am a PhD student in neuroscience at UC Berkeley. I am conducting my thesis research with Dr. William Jagust, using multimodal neuroimaging from MRI and PET to study aging and preclinincal Alzheimer's disease from a network perspective.

I live in Albany, California with my husband James and my daughter Samantha (born November, 2016). I love the outdoors, and enjoy a good nature hike. I also like to get-down in the kitchen. Or on the rock wall. I'm a closet philosopher, so be prepared if you get me chatting...
